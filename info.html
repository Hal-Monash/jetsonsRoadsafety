TOP REASONS FOR ACCIDENTS AMONG THE YOUNG DRIVERS (BASED ON OUR DATA SET)


IN VICTORIA

In the last ten years, 350 young drivers lost their lives in Victoria. In 2016, 19% of the drivers who lost their life are young drivers, but only 10 percent of the drivers are young drivers.

It is worth mentioning that the death numbers of young drivers increased from 44 to 48 from 2016 to 2017.


YOUNG DRIVERS

Young drivers face many challenges when learning the complex task of driving a vehicle. With their inexperience, they also face a higher risk of danger.


WHY FOCUS

According to a recent survey of young drivers by the Australian Transport Safety Bureau (ATBS), it was found that young drivers were at greater risk on the roads for a range of reasons including:

  • lack of experience
  • limited ability and judgement
  • underestimation of risks
  • deliberate risk-taking behaviours
  • use of alcohol and drugs.

DISTRACTED DRIVING

Any distraction while driving can put the lives of others and yours at risk. Young drivers are more likely to take risks, use smartphones and may try to multitask while driving.
